Individual Project

The all-new Siri for Financial Assistance

It's a one-week design challenge. The task is to redesign Siri or Google Assistant to allow users to ask about their personal finance in unbounded and unconstrained ways.

What would Artificial Intelligence do in the future of financial assistance? Creating personalized financial goal, sticking to financial plan, giving insightful analysis in a conversational way... Financial management is extremely important for millennials to get financial independence in their busy daily life.

Can you carry a financial assistant in your pocket? Could you AI assistant tell you when to be care of your spending? What if you have timely suggestions predict what would your future budget be look like? I explore so many different possibilities from users perspective. And I explored this hypothetical Siri for financial assistant.

I did a secondary research on Quora to create a more tailored experience for users, and help to create a step-by-step goal and budget managing experience that is more empathetic to users. I also designed a comprehensive workflow that features conversational UI that shapes everyday experience. The service removes the burden to search and navigate the pages and pages information, helping cater to the great diversity of users’ financial information. This service assists in scheduling upcoming payments, as well as assuring the complex and confusing questions for users in natural language.

Bottom-up

Understanding the Landscape

Competitive Analysis

One biggest challenge is that I was not familiar with personal finance and AI's advantage over traditional app. Competitive Analysis helped me to know what the typical features a financial management app has and understand different types of AI assistants. For example, Siri is more reative compared to Google Assistant. She waitS for you to tell her what you want, while Google Assistant recalls information from previous converstions for context.

Affinity Wall

The second biggest challenge is I couldn't get access to professionals such as financial analysts or sales to interview in such a limited time period. So I browsed Quora to gauge the most typical scenarios where people prefer to use AI assistant. I was able to gather a diversity of ideas and a ton of inspiration from this Q&A platform. For example, people prefer to use Siri when their hands are occupied such as cooking or driving and they prefer to talk to their phone to set the reminder or jot down the notes.

Persona

By considering the daily and typical user scenarios where AI assistant might actually assuage pain in the ass, I wanted to ensure the user journey flows smoothly wherever it goes. I did my best to learn about the typical users' backgrounds, goals, knowledge. The provisional persona helped me to make appropriate guess without sufficient access to them. Persona was also a great communication tool to make everyone is on the same page.

People use Siri or Google Assistant when ...

"I’ll occasionally call up Siri when my hands are otherwise occupied — frequently when I’m cooking or baking in the kitchen."

"She's also good for quick answers to factual questions. Earlier today, I asked her 'How far is Hawaii from California?'

"It’s really handy when I remember I need to pick up something at the store or want to make a note to do something later on."

"I use it frequently when I'm too tired to unlock my phone and manually set up the alarm."

Top-down

How Might We

I made every means to understand users needs, always brought users goals at the first mind. By thinking from the “How Might We” questions, I broke down the big picture to feasible chunks.

How might AI help me keep track of my financial status?

How might AI give me some data insights?

High might AI help me get on the right track of financial plan?

How might we converse with AI in unbounded and unconstrained ways?

User Story

How might AI help me stick to my financial plan?

AI can help them set their goals
  • Break big goals down into smaller, SMART goals
  • Involve others in users’ goals so they’re even more likely to stay accountable
AI can help them stick on their plans

  • Make plans more flexible

  • AI can accommodate to different learning styles (visual, logical, auditory, kinesthetic, and verbal)

User Story

How might AI help me keep track of my financial status?

Ask AI assistant for their personal finance
  • Get value out of complex data
  • Get prediction
Ask AI assistant to monitor their budget
  • Tell AI some complicated trackers
  • Ask AI to get notifications on financial status if the trigger is initiated
  • Make monitoring budget more appealing
  • Allow ongoing comparison between actual results and the plan
AI can anticipate users' needs and bring up information before they ask for it
  • Give personalized predictions based on where users have been (Seattle) or how users used the app (hasn’t checked the balance for 3 days)
  • Send users everyday report or give them some advices when they are driving and cooking

User Story

How might AI give me some data insights?

AI can help users make informed decisions
  • Give users some deep or nuanced insights
  • Point out the most valuable information rather than overwhelm readers by reading all the data
  • Engage users by intuitive interface

User Story

How might we converse with AI in unbounded and unconstrained ways?

AI can understand the context
  • AI can anticipate users' needs and bring up information before they ask for it
  • Walk users through some sample questions which are not so straightforward
AI can help users to ask questions
  • Recognition is better than recall. Have a list of personalized question or keywords to prompt users based on their needs
AI can communicate effectively and naturally
  • Use the format [suggestion+reason+supportive data+risk or benefit] to communicate effectively
  • Avoid jargon or technical language

Reflection

Final Thoughts

Today Artificial Intelligence is a buzzword for interaction designers. Since the traditional banking interfaces are usually terribly complex, it seems to be the perfect tool to simply the experience and improve customers’ engagement. But AI assistant should do more than just giving answers. It should empower users by:

  • Helping people understand what system they are following
  • Giving some new insights that people have never thought about
  • Helping people make informed decision

I explored the notion that AI can help you get on track of financial plan more proactively, by breaking down the intimidating financial goal to chunkable daily goals. I prototyped the interfaces that can give users more nuanced insights by more effective and natural communication format [suggestion + reason + supportive data + risk and benefit].

To make the AI more proactive, I also explored the notion that AI can understand the context, anticipate users’ needs, and bring up information before they even ask for it. By actively sending timely notifications and daily report to users, it complements users existing daily behavioral patterns, form new habits, and create unique contextual experiences for users in their daily lives.

Designing Siri for financial assistant offers a host of design challenges and possibilities for young designers like me, and I won the hearts of the chief designer and CEO by creating an engaging human-centered experience.
