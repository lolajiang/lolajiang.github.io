Individual Project

Is this the future of Mental Health?

Mindfulness App is a scientific program-based app designed for people who have emotion disorders to learn emotion control techniques effectively.

Major depression is one of the most common mental illnesses, affecting 6.7% (more than 16 million) of American adults each year (Rockville, 2014). Depression causes people to lose pleasure from daily life, can complicate other medical conditions, and can even be serious enough to lead to suicide. Mindfulness has been proved to be effective in treating depression (Stacy, 2015), but there are four main issues:

  • It’s hard to keep on practicing every day.
  • Novices tend to get distracted easily when practicing.
  • It’s hard to break the habits and apply mindfulness in action.
  • Users want a positive community to support themselves.

Mindfulness App is an application that help people who have emotion disorders to learn emotion control techniques. The engagement loop is composed of four parts:

  • Professional Guides: The glue that allows people to learn not only the techniques, but life attitudes, in a professional platform without hassle.
  • Support and Sharing: Start from experienced practitioners, building up a ripple effect within the learning community for a more confident self.
  • Scientific Feedback: Support people with more intuitive and rational tools.
  • Personalization and Reflection: To fully engage people, learning experience needs to rely on people’s tastes and progression.

Research

Understanding the Landscape

I discovered a review (Mani M, 2015) that conducted a systematic review of mindfulness-based apps and pointed out several features of selected high-quality mindfulness apps, such as providing guided meditations, timers & reminders, educational videos, and app community.

Then I conducted a usability testing for three high-quality apps by visiting a mindfulness-training classroom and led 5 young female through a series of design research exercises. Talking to those people who suffered from Premenstrual syndrome (PMS) gave me a better understanding of their preferences of different featuers and the main issues they were struggling with: they provide me surprising insights that they generally like more aesthetical design despite of its complex information hierarchies. Moreover, they appreciated the progressive training programs that could help them to incorporate mindfulness into their daily lives.

I also talked to two depressed parents in order to understand how they perceive their mental illness and the methods they've tried. Recognizing that patients felt overwhelmed by the negative thoughts and found it hard to pick the right technique for the emotion in that specific situation, I came up with a set of design guidelines:

  • Knowledge Base: Help patients to build their repertoire of techniques to deal with emotion fluctuations in daily life.
  • Support Group: Share their experience of applying the techniques in their daily lives. Moderators are necessary because they can guide the group or the community to build a positive atmosphere.
  • Individual Counsoler: Solve the problems they meet and learn the techniques better.

Ideation

From the Big Picture to the Details

Sketching Alternatives

I appreciated this methodology because it not only helped to stretch myself to think of unique solutions to my problem but also served as a icebreaker and gave interviewees clue to talk about. The interviewees loved the cartoonish style of my character, a little guy with a cloud on his head.

Brainstorming

To tackle the untapped desires, I generated a lot of ideas (30 ideas) by staying focused on a single opportunity area. To generate this large number of ideas, I tried out each of the techniques I learned in the User Experience: Research & Prototyping: listing ideas, eliminating a constraint, making an analogy, exaggerating, and getting in the garbage. The goal here is to diverge, or generate as many ideas as possible. I give myself the safe space to be creative without judgment.

Card Sorting

To prioritize the features, I conducted a card sorting exercise with 5 people to learn about what features are important to users when they try to learn about a product and potentially get it. I provided 23 cards with a feature and a brief description written on each and asked each participant to arrange them in three decks (Favourite, So-so, Dislike). To further understand their needs, I asked them to arrange the Favourite deck in the order of importance.

Synthesis

Defining Principles to Drive Design Decisions

The card sorting results helped me sort features that users really care. I believe the experience would be more desirable when it gives people what they need not everything it can provide.

Top 5 favourite features are:

  1. Activities (What am I doing/ Where am I)
  2. Program-based Training
  3. Health Action Process Approach
  4. Personalized Mandala
  5. Coach

Users wanted a scientific and systematic training program. They also focused on focused on HAPA principles which can enhance their efficacy and planning. The most interesting one is Personalized Mandala, a flower that will grow with users’ progress and will wither if they give up practicing.

“I feel a sense of spirituality from the mandala. Besides, I love Simulation Game of growing plants, because it helps me to realize my responsibility and will encourage me to keep practicing.” —User 5

Top 5 dislike features are:

  1. Group for Friends
  2. Message Me! Communicate with Guru (Sakyamuni)
  3. Scrapbook for Collection
  4. MBCT-based Solutions to Negative Thoughts
  5. Portable EEG

Users disregarded Group for Friends. They thought the practicing is a more intimate journey of self-exploration. Some of them were struggling with personal affairs like anxiety or depression. So it’s important for the app to provide the password lock rather than share button. Besides, some users didn’t like Portable EEG. They were not accustomed to wearing a clumsy hat to record their brain waves. On the contrary, Vital Signs Camera which can record breath counts was more popular because it’s less intrusive and more convenient. One mistake I made was that most users didn’t understand what MBCT-based Solutions to Negative Thoughts mean based on my description.

“I don’t need an electronic gadget to record my data because it’s strange to wear a hat on my head. It’s easy for me to recognize that I’m in the state of mindfulness. Peaceful and comfortable. But I think the function of recording breath counts or heart rates is a must to understand myself better.” — User 4

Iteration

Hashing Out the Design Details

Feature Scoping

To imagine what users can do with the mindfulness app and break it down into digestible tasks to be done, I did a feature scoping. This helped me to identify features necessary to alleviate the pain points. The engagement loop is composed of four parts:

  • Professional Guides
  • Support and Sharing
  • Scientific Feedback
  • Personalization and Reflection

Early Sketches

I began with a lot of sketches. From early ones with only boxes and lines all the way to some detailed sketches with icons and graphics in it. I was impressed by the design of MUJI to Relax and Pause, and I tried to make a same feeling of my app. I also created a pinterest board to gather my ideas.

Then I converted the sketches to digital versions and linked them in Axure to create a clickable prototype.

Usability Testing

To figure out the information architecture and the layouts of the icons before digging deep into the nitty gritty so early, I did three usability testings with my friends. Based on their suggestions, I decided to make the hierarchy more intuitive for users and redesign the user interface to make the application more self-explainatory.

Because of some limitations in Axure, I decided to use Pixate to communicate the app’s interactions and transitions. The transitions in the app were chosen to create a sense of intimacy like card swiping. Because Pixate offers all the native Android transitions, it was the perfect tool at my disposal.
Soon after I regretted using Pixate to design transitions so early, especially I was still in a low-fidelity stage. I should figure out the information architecture and the layouts of the icons before I dig deep into the nitty gritty so early.
Add Navigation Bar
  • Make the hierarchy more intuitive.
  • Add texts to each icon for users to navigate effortlessly.
Use Tab Bars
  • To flatten the information architecture.
  • For users to understand the relationship between different sections (course, tools and group)
  • For users to quickly switch between different sections
Redesign the Course Page
  • Flesh out the courses by adding videos, articles, daily practices and diary.
  • Replace the calendar with a syllabus so that users can review the lessons seamlessly.
Redesign the Tools Page
  • Replace the upper arrow with a separate tab bar to call out the panel.
  • Add descriptions so that users will be informed why these practices are important.
Based on the usability testings, some design changes are:
  • Discard the upper arrow to call out the panel for techniques.
  • Add texts to each icon for users to navigate effortlessly.
  • Redesign the daily tasks to make them different from onboarding pictures.
  • Add tutorials to guide users how to navigate the app if the app can’t self explain itself.
  • Make the hierarchy more intuitive for users to figure out the relationship between daily tasks, technique panels and program.

Rationalization

Why Using Mindfulness App as a Training Tool?

Professional Guides

Mindfulness app provides scientific-based program to help users to learn the most effective relapse-prevention treatment for depression. This not only help patients to build their repertoire of techniques to deal with emotion fluctuations in daily life, but also the right life attitudes to deal with ups and downs.

Support and Sharing

To mimic the friendly atmosphere of the support group, mindfulness app provides the question and answer platform to build up a ripple effect within the learning community for a more confident self. Experienced moderators are necessary to guide the group from relapsing.

Scientific Feedback

Novices often finds it difficult to keep on practising without right feedback. Mindfulness app makes use of breaths to detect whether the users focus on practising or not. By supporting people with more intuitive and rational tools, my app supports patients to break their habits and apply mindfulness in action.

Personalization and Reflection

Mindfulness app encourages patients to reflect on their emotions and thought through diary. To fully engage people, people’s learning experiences are personalized based on their tastes and progression.

Reflection

Final Thoughts

With scientifically proven mindfulness meditation techniques, My app will guide users through a journey to discover a new method to life. It not only has a program-based training system, but also has day-to-day short guided exercises to help users apply this emotion control technique in action. Moreover, our support groups allow us to learn more effectively and get more feedback with professional guidance. This app has the potential to help people fight anxiety and depression better.

However, never assume my design is good enough. Seek out the most critical person to get their feedback on my work. During my first iteration, I wanted to create a sense of gamification in my low-fidelity prototype, but my instructor pointed out directly my information architecture was confusing and users would get lost in various icons without texts.

With the second iteration, I chose to rely on the Material Design framework to prioritize different functions so that users can navigate effortlessly. But on the final presentation, my classmates pointed out they like the idea of using different growth period of Mandala to represent learning progress. Those complaints boiled down to one thing - lack of individuality. People tend to seek intimacy or spirituality from this app.

As Julie Zhuo mentioned, a design can be easily understood and feel well-crafted. My third iteration is taking shape, focusing more on visual design to enhance the sense of intimacy.
