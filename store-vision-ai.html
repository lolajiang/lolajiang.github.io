Optimizing Store Operation with AI

Spearheaded the design of AI-powered user journeys, optimizing store operations, reducing out-of-stock rates by 10% in test stores, and saving an estimated $15M in revenue. Pioneered a strategic human-in-the-loop framework, optimizing data labeling tasks for a 91.7% CSAT.

Background

With 100 million IP cameras shipped annually, computer vision technology holds tremendous potential for retailers. Recognizing the need for a fresh perspective in the retail industry, Store Vision AI Services aimed to enhance customer interactions, streamline operations, and stay ahead of the competition. Collaborating with leading retailers, we redefined the in-store experience to help store crews to improve their store operation.

Research

The project began without a well-defined vision for how the store should operate. To address this, I partnered with our researcher, Obinna, to directly observe store managers and crews.

Then I translated the insights into journey mapping and storyboards to fully explore the problem space:

Product ecosystem

I designed both in-store and data labeling experiences, creating a unified ecosystem.

Store experience

Store crews leverage AI to prevent out-of-stock events and optimize store operations.

Data labeling experience

Human labeling enhances the accuracy and performance of AI models.

Store Experience

Deep research and journey mapping illuminated the entire customer experience, pinpointing opportunities for innovation at every stage. This led to a holistic, user-focused solution. Our comprehensive service design approach uncovered three key improvement areas:

  • Building AI Trust: How might we foster realistic expectations in AI?
  • Seamless Adoption: How might we help store crews onboard easily?
  • Team Collaboration: How might we optimize collaboration among store staff?

AI Onboarding: Setting Realistic Expectations

During field research, I discovered store managers were eager for technology to transform their operations. It's essential to balance that enthusiasm with realistic expectations. We must avoid overpromising "AI magic" and instead highlight how AI and human expertise work best together.

To encourage this collaborative mindset, I designed the homepage to emphasize this partnership. Store managers see upfront that while our AI is powerful, it relies on their feedback and industry knowledge (like seasonal trends) to continuously improve.

Improved Store Operations

Priority tabs streamline store operations for managers, focusing on their top concern: preventing out-of-stock situations. To foster trust, I balance powerful AI with transparency. Camera views are emphasized, allowing managers to verify results easily. If needed, they can provide feedback, helping the AI continually learn and tackle even challenging scenarios like fridges or enclosed shelves.

Data labeler experience

Our advanced ML models rely on human-in-the-loop feedback for optimal results. Recognizing the need for fast, accurate data labeling, I took the initiative to develop and test design hypotheses, even without a formal PRD. This proactive approach paid off - user research validated the proposed designs, providing a strong foundation for further development.

Our service blueprint revealed three core areas for improvement:

  • Boosting Productivity: How might we make data labeling more efficient?
  • Fostering Motivation: How might we keep labelers engaged?
  • Promoting Well-Being: How might we ensure a positive experience?

1. Boosting Productivity

Iteration 1: Understanding Labeling Tasks: Bulk or Single?

To balance data labeling speed and accuracy, I employed a research-backed approach. User testing helped pinpoint tasks suitable for efficient bulk labeling (like detecting shape and color across a shelf) versus those requiring a slower, detailed view. This optimized the human-in-the-loop process, boosting both quality and efficiency.

Option 2: one-by-one view is preferred for in-depth tasks like shelf health analysis.
On the other hand, simple tasks, like checking shape and color, can be done quickly on in bulk.

Iteration 2: Choosing the Right AI Assistance

My second round of testing explored how AI assistance could boost both the speed and accuracy of data labeling. I tested several levels of assistance:

  1. No AI: A baseline for comparison.
  2. Feature Explanation: Visual cues about how the AI analyzes stock levels.
  3. Example-Based: Showing typical images of different stock levels.
  4. Predicted Label (No Confidence): AI's suggested label without accuracy score.
  5. Predicted Label (With Confidence): Suggested label plus a confidence score.
  6. Combination: Using features, examples, and predicted labels together.
Several levels of AI assitance to boost both the speed and accuracy of data labeling

Results validated my hypotheses:

  • Explanations alone offered minor improvement.
  • Predicted labels significantly boosted performance over explanations.
  • Adding a confidence score further increased speed and accuracy.

Iteration 3: Insights That Boost User Performance

To further streamline the process, I explored how insights from Generative AI could be incorporated. I considered two options:

  1. Chat UI: Users could ask the AI questions directly in a chat window.
  2. Insights Panel: Key insights displayed prominently on the screen.

Research showed the Insights Panel was the clear winner. It offers immediate information with minimal effort, enhancing labeler focus and performance.

2. Fostering Motivation

This is the Experience Fluctuation Model. This model shows how people react differently to task difficulty based on their skill level.

To optimize the labeling experience, we might "level up" the task difficulty when users are performing well. This carefully matches their growing skills with greater challenges, leading to a state of "flow" - optimum focus and engagement.

Personalized Progress

The AI tracks individual performance, offering skill-building challenges at the right time for continuous growth.

Proactive Guidance

The AI recognizes patterns in performance, suggesting breaks or resources when needed to optimize the workflow.

3. Promoting Well-being

Data labeling work can be repetitive. To combat fatigue and boost wellbeing, I focused on:

  • Purpose: Reinforcing how each task contributes to bigger goals, building a sense of meaning.
  • Achievement: Publicly recognizing wins and celebrating individual progress to increase motivation.
  • Healthy Practices: Promoting breaks and providing resources to help labelers refresh and re-energize.

Meaningful Moments

Provide engaging insights throughout the process, connecting labelers to the greater impact of their work.

Celebrate Success

Celebrating success means recognizing those who go above and beyond, showcasing their wins, and fostering a culture of achievement.

Healthy Practices

Helping labelers avoid burnout by proactively suggesting rest periods after long stints of focused work.

  • Executive Endorsement: Positive buzz fueled by executive spotlights on social media and a feature in the Wall Street Journal.
  • Proven User Satisfaction: Achieved an impressive 91.7% CSAT on data labeling tasks, demonstrating gains in both efficiency and accuracy.
