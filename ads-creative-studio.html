Information architecture

Challenge: Bridging the gap between video and display ad workflows

Reconciling the distinct workflows of video and display ads proved to be a major hurdle. Initially, we operated under the mistaken assumption that video users prioritize defining rules before exploring variants, due to the higher production cost of video ads. Display users, conversely, preferred the opposite flow, as display ads are quicker and cheaper to produce. This fundamental difference in mental models and data structures posed a significant challenge to achieving alignment.

Solution: Unearthing user-centric insights

To overcome this roadblock, I delved into past research and user testing findings. This comprehensive review unearthed valuable evidence of the diverse ways users collaborate within our platform. These insights underscored the critical need for an information architecture that embraced this inherent flexibility.

Buyin: Visualization and stakeholder alignment

I then spearheaded the development of a strategic UX/product framework to accommodate these workflow discrepancies. Through focused discussions with key stakeholders, I addressed their concerns and ensured buy-in for this user-centric approach.

Outcome: A unified information architecture for diverse needs

This commitment to flexibility resulted in a platform that effectively caters to the distinct needs of both video and display ad users, while maintaining a coherent and streamlined UX. The meticulous process of harmonizing seemingly disparate workflows culminated in a unified platform that seamlessly integrates the creative and media user experiences.

Information architecture supports flexible collaboration.

Final design

Template: Establish Swappable Elements for Strategic Ad Personalization

Using a visual-first workflow, users can seamlessly construct and preview ad templates by incorporating various elements and fine-tuning styles. This process allows them to identify which elements can be swapped out. For instance, the base video can be swapped to generate different video variations tailored to specific audiences. The remaining three elements (logo, message, and voiceover) remain constant, ensuring consistency across ad variations. This approach enhances creative users' understanding of the dynamic strategy.

Variants: Unleash Creative Potential with Ease

Users can effortlessly create ad variations on the Variants page. Swappable elements are conveniently displayed on the right panel, empowering brands and creative teams to craft and test unique display and video formats with ease.

Rules: Unravel Dynamic Strategy Complexities

The Rules tab serves as the central hub for advertisers to define their targeting criteria, encompassing factors such as audience type, geographic location, date and time parameters, and more. These targeting signals can then be seamlessly assigned to one or more ad variations

Review: Effortless Quality Control

Finally, the Review tab provides a comprehensive platform for advertisers to meticulously examine ad variations generated based on diverse targeting rules. This centralized review process ensures that the right ad variations are precisely matched to their intended audience.
