Redesigning AI training for everyone

I was the sole web designer for a leading conversational AI training platform for bank accounts. Making AI training make sense to people, so they can train AI easily.

Clinc builds the most advanced conversational AI platform for financial services, and delivering enterprise grade AI solutions to the world's leading banking and financial industry. It’s used by companies like USAA, and gives users the power to have a new and revolutionary conversational AI experience with their financial information.

As the only designer working on Clinc's AI training platform, I led the project to revamp the visual style and solve the key usability issues by continuously re-imagining what it’s like for Clinc's users to train AI. I'll share my learning on how I understand user’s mental model and break down the barriers to help them train AI easily.

How to Start a Design System and Construct it?

For the design system, I wanted to mature the Clinc brand and take the visual style from something that was very bootstrap like to incorporating more vivid and mirroring the visual style of our main website.

After I created a basic style guide, I began mockup up the screens in code by creating new stylesheets. Design by coding made it really easy for me to show in real-time what projects might look like to stakeholders. This approached was much faster and allowed for quick iteration.

Why the Old Training Website Fails and How to Fix it?

Helping people train an AI is hard. The old website has all the features splattered around without clear information architecture for power users or helpful guidance for non-power users. People usually end up being stuck in the middle, confronted with an error or a failure, with no confidence to go through the whole daunting process.

An example of poor usability for power users. The navigation is so cumbersome even for the power users. They are also less confident to train the competency due to the insufficient feedback.
  1. Navigation Issue
    Power users have to switch back and forth between sidebar and work area to make changes. They want a smoother flow to add a new competency.
  2. Insufficient Feedback
    All the training data have been listed, some of which are irrelevant to the new competency. Power users have to constantly check if they are doing changes on the right competency.

Confusing Terminologies

"I have no idea what's the difference between ‘responses’ and ‘config file’."

An example of poor usability for non-power users. Besides the two usability issues faced with power users, the training process also confuses and frustates them.
  1. Navigation Issue
    The sidebar doesn't make much sense to non-power users. One user didn’t notice that he can switch to different pages with the help of the sidebar. Instead, he constantly used the back and forward buttons in the web browser to navigate.
  2. Insufficient Feedback
    Non-power users can't receive enought feedback of what to do next after they add several training data. One user tried to hit the training button, which would cause problem due to the incomplete training process.
  3. Unclear Process
    Non-power users have no idea what's the training process. The flow is confusing to them. As one user said:“It’s hard to know what to do next, the flow is confusing.”
  4. Confusing Terminologies
    Terminology is another hindrance for non-power users. Although they kind of grasp the basic concepts of competency, state, classification, slot and utterance, to apply the understanding to the training is another story.

Based on the research, I worked on figuring out some goals that would guide me through my design journey. I raised two "How Might We" questions to solve the problem:

  • How might we help users understand the training process?
  • How might we help users gather key info?

How might we help users understand the training process?

At the moment, people can't rely on the sidebar to navigate the training process. How to make sense of the complex system? How to communicate the training process to people? How to apply the previous learnings to improve the flow? Also, how to break down the barriers?

I then started exploring what the ideal training experience could be. After a few brainstorming sessions, I came up with a few ideas that I started designing and prototyping: steppers, a training guide section, tips, navigation bar, training guide page…

  1. Training process is an iterative process, while the steppers fail to give users much freedom to go back and forth between steps.
  2. Strike a perfect balance between navigation and flexibility. Users won't lose context when they go back and forth between steps and get the job done.
A few concepts I prototyped to guide users through the training process.

How might we help users gather key info?

As I was making progress on the training, I was also thinking about what would be an easy way for people to gather key info so that they can make informed decisions. Meanwhile, I decided to solve the issue of poor information architecture, and started discovering the reasons broke the navigation experience.

One thing caught my attention. There was one page with all the relevant key info was hidden so deep for users to get access to. That's why in the usability testing one user constantly used the back and forward buttons in the web browser to get back to this page.

  1. The most interesting one with flatter information architecture. Users can see all the related info at a glance without digging deep into the website. However, is it the best choice to put the info graph in the State Graph Page?
A few concepts I prototyped to help users get key info easily.

Then I had the outside-the-box idea of offering an overview page. Users can see all the related info at a glance without digging deep into the website. A perfect solution! I also find a way of displaying the latest created competency at the top. I made use of card design that serves as an entry point to the training guide page.

New Overview Page. Users can keep track of the training progress easily.

  2. List the in-progress competency at the top.
  3. Inform users the states of training and what to do next.
  4. Users can see all the related info at a glance without digging deep into the website.

The Challenge

The biggest challenge for this project was navigating the problem space. The task of “building a design system” sounded so daunting at first compared to how comprehensive and complicated the iOS design system or the Google Material Design system are. I am glad my design hit the bar.

AI training itself is also a new problem space for me. I had to keep bugging my colleagues to understand how it actually works. With this said, I got the chance to work with so many amazingly talented individuals before I left, so it was definitely worth it.
